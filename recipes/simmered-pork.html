Simmered pork

Chasu-style pork

This pork simmered in broth can be used as a topping for any noodle dish, with rice or in a lunch box. It's one of the most popular ingredients in asian food.

Ingredients

  • 1 leek
  • 72 g raw sugar
  • 600 g pork, marbled
  • 1,5 tablespoons of oyster sauce
  • 4 boiled eggs (you can use both hard and soft boiled)
  • Small piece of ginger (add to your liking)
  • 200 ml sake
  • 210 ml soy sauce
  • 1 L water

Steps

  1. Cut the vegetables and tge pork in 2-3 pieces and put in a pan. The pan must be able to hold the pork in one layer.
  2. Put the rest of ingredients in the pan, but leave the eggs out.
  3. Once boiling, put on the small heat.
  4. Let it simmer with the lid on for about 30 minutes.
  5. Take the lid off and let it cook for about 25-35 minutes.
  6. Take the pork out and let the broth cook until it has consistency of sauce.
  7. Put the meat and eggs back in the pan and cover with the sauce.
  8. Take it off the stove and let it rest for a bit.

And you are done! Slice it into fewer pieces if you want to use it as topping.
